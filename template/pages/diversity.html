diversity

the diversity committee

our mission: To promote an inclusive environment in the PostgreSQL community through outreach programs and the support of educational endeavors for underrepresented PostgreSQL users in the United States.

our priority: inclusion. Why? because inclusive communities help to reduce and eliminate barriers to participation, and we want everyone to participate in the creation and innovation of software.

what's the key? We believe that open-source software and communities are key for fostering inclusion in information technology. Through community engagement, development, and support, as well as a philosophy that everyone is welcome, equal, and has a role, open-source software communities are working to reduce and eliminate barriers to participation in information technology.

it all comes together. Open Source is not just about permissive licenses that reduce economic barriers to using the software - itâ€™s also about providing opportunities for everyone to participate in software development, research, advocacy, and governance. We whole-heartedly believe in and support this at PgUS and consistently seek to create and support these opportunities for participation through the work of the Diversity Committee, and beyond!

talk postgresql online

The PostgreSQL community can be found a lot of places - come interact with us! You can find us on:

  • IRC
  • Slack
  • Twitter

You can find other ways of contacting the PostgreSQL community about topics such as security issues, found bugs, and press inquiries on the Contact page on the main PostgreSQL website.

There are also more than a dozen mailing lists available, with topics including:

  • Announcements
  • Development
  • Jobs

View all information regarding current PostgreSQL mailing lists and how to manage your subscriptions to them here.

development resources

For a wide range of user generated content, including specific information on contributing code to PostgreSQL, check out the PostgreSQL wiki. Additionally, official documentation for PostgreSQL can be found for every version of PostgreSQL since it's creation - take a look!

Find community supported lists of online resources, books, and frequently asked questions on the main PostgreSQL website.

Interested in reading more discussions and experiments around PostgreSQL use? Check out Planet PostgreSQL, a blog aggregator of PostgreSQL related topics from across the web.

Finally, you can find more information specifically on PostgreSQL coding for developers on the main PostgreSQL website; this includes links to navigate the source code, search for git commit hashs, view a backend flowchart, and more!

postgresql events

local postgresql user groups - These amazing resources can be found nearly everywhere around the world. If there isn't one currently meeting, we can help you set one up in your area to promote the learning and collaborative discussion of PostgreSQL in local settings near you - just contact diversity@postgresql.us! For information on which user groups are currently meeting, go to the official PostgreSQL website to view the full list of local PUGs sorted by country and local region.

upcoming community events - The PostgreSQL community loves to get together and promote interactive discussions and activities around PostgreSQL learning, development, and networking. Join us at both in person and virtual events - you can find a full list of all PostgreSQL community events on the main PostgreSQL website!

in need of financial assistance? We may be able to help! We offer financial scholarships for diverse candidates thanks to generous donations from our sponsors and other independent contributors. View our Diversity Scholarship offering below for more information.

diversity scholarship

what is this? An opportunity for diverse candidates to receive financial assistance for travelling or attending virtual or in-person events, with the intention of presenting, learning, or connecting in the PostgreSQL community!

how to apply? Applications are evaluated on a case-by-case basis; you must be applying to attend any PostgreSQL specific event, such as a user group or community edorsed conference. For more information and to actually apply for the PgUS Diversity Scholarship, please visit the Google Form and fill out our application!

can you help us?

interested in working with the diversity committee? Help us in our efforts to promote diversity and inclusion in the PostgreSQL community and beyond - we would love your participation! You can request to join our Diversity Committee here, or if you'd prefer to volunteer your time on specific tasks (from everything ranging from writing, marketing, coding, design, video, recording, mentorship, or anything else you can think of), you can request to be on our volunteer list!

community diversity opportunities are available - everywhere around the world, there's a local PostgreSQL group seeking volunteers or recruits for specific opportunities to help the PostgreSQL community. Are you available to help? Contact us at diversity@postgresql.us if you're able to help with any of these open positions!
